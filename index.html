It's fair to say that ADHD is a hard disorder to understand. Most people know the basics, but there is a lot more going on behind the scenes than most realize. Here are 10 facts you may or may not have known about ADHD!

About Me and ADHD!"

I was diagnosed with ADHD at 12 and I'm one of the many lucky adults who gets to keep it through adulthood!
An environment I have a really hard time in is the classroom. Be it in-person, virtual, or self-paced curriculum, things just don't click as fast as they do for others. Nothing gets my anxiety going better than a teacher announcing,

"Okay. Everybody take about five minutes to read what the author wrote and then we will all discuss!">

"Five minutes! You expect me to be able to talk about all this after just five minutes?"

"Hmm, looks like everyone else is just soaking up everything on the page?"

"Quick, just look at the page and act like you're reading. Grab a couple key words and do your best to fumble through an answer if the instructor calls on you".

Most people have a good idea of some of the symptoms for ADHD: losing focus or trailing off while someone is talking to you; needing to move around a lot or fidget. What many don't realize, is that it is a legit mental health condition caused from actually having a brain that operates differently than a neurotypical brain. To make it more confusing, ADHD is not just one disorder. It carries with it a smorgasbord of unfortunate, disruptive disorders that make functioning in this built for normal world a real challenge.
